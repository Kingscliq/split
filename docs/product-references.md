# Product References

This file captures external products worth reviewing for Split product thinking. These are references for customer insights, positioning, onboarding, and support lessons. They are not requirements for the Split MVP.

## App Store References

### Split Pay

Link: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/split-pay/id1484143875

Positioning:

- Focuses on group dining and bill splitting.
- Core flow includes receipt capture, assigning items to friends, calculating tip/tax, and settling through payment integrations.
- Messaging emphasizes reducing awkwardness and making fair splitting easier.

Useful customer-success insights:

- Users value transparency because everyone can see the bill and understand what they owe.
- Users complain strongly when the main flow breaks, especially receipt upload/capture.
- A reachable support channel matters; a bounced support email created a bad customer experience.
- "No account or installation required" for participants is a strong adoption lever.
- Recent contacts and live group status reduce repeated setup friction.

Relevance to Split:

- Split should make payment status transparent and easy to verify.
- Split should keep participant flow lightweight from shared links.
- Split should treat support/recovery states as product features, not afterthoughts.
- Split should avoid brittle flows where one failure blocks the whole experience.

### SplitIt: Group Bill Splitter

Link: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/splitit-group-bill-splitter/id6472703319

Positioning:

- Focuses on scanning receipts, assigning items, tracking group expenses, and settling balances.
- Category is Finance and the app monetizes through in-app purchases.
- Strong app rating suggests users respond well to a smooth, low-friction splitting workflow.

Useful customer-success insights:

- Users want to avoid spreadsheets and manual math.
- Receipt scanning is valued, but users also ask for manual correction and multiple-photo support.
- Groups, balances, recent users, different payment platforms, and onboarding improvements appear in the product evolution.
- Users appreciate being able to share summaries into group chats.
- Privacy expectations matter because contacts, identifiers, and user content may be involved.

Relevance to Split:

- Split should lead with clarity and low mental effort.
- Split should support group-chat sharing well.
- Split should make the "who has paid / who is pending" state obvious.
- Split should avoid collecting unnecessary personal data in MVP.
- Advanced receipt parsing, groups, and social features should remain future scope.

## Product Lessons For Split

- Keep MVP centered on create, share, pay, verify.
- Reduce awkward follow-up by making the status page the shared truth.
- Prioritize mobile and group-chat sharing.
- Make failure states clear: wallet not connected, wrong wallet, rejected signature, failed transaction, stale status.
- Keep support contact and troubleshooting visible in docs before public testing.
- Do not make receipt OCR, itemized restaurant splitting, recurring groups, or social feeds part of MVP.
